OPERATIONAL RISKS

RISKS ENCOUNTERED DURING TRANSPORTATION AT SEA

Collisions

The most serious outcomes of navigational failures are ship collisions.

Fires

Fires onboard are among the most dangerous risks in the maritime industry.

Terrorist Activities

Maritime routes are vulnerable to use for terrorist fundraising and logistics.

Bad Weather Conditions

Climate change is intensifying extreme weather events, impacting global shipping.

Failures in Cargo System

Accidents involving cargo can occur during loading, transit, or unloading stages.

RISKS ENCOUNTERED DURING LOADING-UNLOADING OPERATIONS

HUMAN RISKS

Various concerns, including fatigue, ineffective communication, insufficient knowledge of ship systems, limited automation, incomplete information, flawed decision-making, and poor maintenance, can result in operational errors that lead to accidents.

BAD WEATHER CONDITIONS

Port operations are significantly impacted by severe weather occurrences, particularly when it comes to cargo loading and unloading.

FAILURES OF CONSTRUCTION MACHINES

The shipment of heavy machinery involves inherent risks, particularly the potential for mechanical breakdowns during transit.

LASHING FAILURES

Lashing plays a crucial role in ensuring the stability of cargo and minimizing damage during transportation.

SOFTWARE FAILURES

The International Maritime Organization (IMO) recognizes the growing threat posed by cyber risks and software malfunctions in maritime operations.

DISASTER

Environmental risks such as severe weather events, natural disasters, and pollution present ongoing challenges for the industry.
