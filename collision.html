OPERATIONAL RISKS

RISKS ENCOUNTERED DURING TRANSPORTATION AT SEA

COLLISIONS

According to Kolodny (2019), navigational errors remain the most common cause of accidents at sea. Despite the availability of modern navigational technologies designed to simplify maritime operations, navigating open waters continues to pose significant risks. These risks can lead to severe consequences, including vessel damage, injuries, and even loss of life.

Among the most serious outcomes of navigational failures are ship collisions, which represent a persistent and critical concern in maritime operations. Chen et. al (2020) emphasizes that such incidents often result in devastating financial losses, environmental harm, and tragic human casualties.

Central to improving maritime safety is addressing the role of human error, which remains a dominant factor in many accidents. As Chen et al. (2020) argue, human input is indispensable in the decision-making process during complex navigational scenarios.
